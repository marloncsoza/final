Gordons bay

.

Gordon's Bay (Afrikaans: Gordonsbaai) is a harbour town in the Western Cape province of South Africa. It is included in the City of Cape Town metropolitan municipality as a suburb of the Helderberg region (formerly called Hottentots Holland). It is situated on the shores of Gordon's Bay in the northeastern corner of False Bay about 58 km from Cape Town.

The first thing you are likely to notice when you go to Gordon’s Bay is the giant anchor and the initials GB on the mountainside overlooking the harbour. Interestingly, these initials don’t stand for Gordon’s Bay, but rather for General Botha, which was the original name of the Naval College named after General Louis Botha who was the first premier of the Union of South Africa.
Fun fact: Did you know that Gordon’s Bay is the only town in South Africa with two working harbours?

Why visit Gordon's Bay?
